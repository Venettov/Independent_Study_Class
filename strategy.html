Graduate Independent Study

Population & Economic Dynamics in Puerto Rico

A graduate research project examining demographic shifts, out-migration, and sustainable development strategies through linked data analysis and policy synthesis.

Synthesis & Strategic Solutions: Puerto Rico‚Äôs demographic and economic challenges reinforce one another. The goal here is to connect the data to concrete, locally focused actions that can stabilize population trends, expand opportunity, and rebuild resilience.

 

Synthesis and Recommendations

Interconnectedness of the crisis. Structural economic vulnerabilities and policy legacies made it hard for the economy to sustain its population, amplifying out-migration and aging and feeding a cycle of contraction and fiscal stress3, 5.

Strategic outlook. The emphasis should shift from externally dependent industries toward a resilient, local economic base. Reversing youth out-migration requires better jobs and targeted investments in social services (education, childcare, health) that raise attachment to place and expand opportunity1, 9.

Priority 1: Grow local industry

  • Support SMEs in tradable services, agro-tech, and cultural/tourism clusters.
  • Procurement pipelines that preference Puerto Rico-based suppliers.
  • Targeted export assistance and logistics upgrades.

Priority 2: Retain & attract talent

  • Early-career fellowships tied to local employers and municipalities.
  • Stackable credentials aligned with in-demand skills.
  • Childcare/transport subsidies to raise labor-force participation.

Priority 3: Social infrastructure

  • School & health anchors as neighborhood hubs.
  • Invest in hazard-resilient housing and public works.

Priority 4: Make migration a choice

  • Career ladders and wages that compete with mainland options.
  • ‚ÄúReturn to PR‚Äù pathways for mid-career Puerto Ricans abroad.

Linking Data Analysis to Strategic Solutions

The historic dependence on outside industries‚Äîincluding the ¬ß936 era‚Äîleft Puerto Rico trailing the mainland and vulnerable to shocks3, 4. The resulting cycle of demographic decline and economic contraction persists as young workers continue to exit5, 9, 10, 11. A durable solution focuses on strengthening a locally rooted economy and the social supports that keep families in Puerto Rico1.

What analysis can do

  • Model workforce & tax-base impacts from youth out-migration10, 11, 5.
  • Map poverty & inequality to target investments17.
  • Trace sectoral impacts of ¬ß936 phase-out to guide new cluster strategy3, 4.
  • Quantify social costs (e.g., school closures) of depopulation9.

Policy levers

  • Local procurement + export promotion for PR-based firms.
  • Apprenticeships & return-migration incentives.
  • Childcare/health/transport programs to lift participation.

Resilience lens

  • Invest where poverty and hazard risk overlap17, 18.
  • Design housing & infrastructure for hazard exposure.
