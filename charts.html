Population & Economic Dynamics in Puerto Rico — Literature Review

Synthesizing peer-reviewed research, government data, and policy analyses on demographic, economic, and social transformations shaping Puerto Rico’s trajectory.

Population Dynamics ¹²

Migration has emerged as the key driver of rapid population aging¹, intensified after Hurricane Maria². The island’s Total Fertility Rate stands at just 0.92³, among the lowest worldwide, while the median age has surpassed 45⁴. Declines in enrollment reflect demographic shrinkage and youth out-migration⁵.

Economic Structure & Performance ³⁴⁵

The phase-out of Section 936 undermined manufacturing stability³, eroding employment and fueling out-migration. Studies highlight persistent wage gaps and weak consumer demand⁵. Economic contraction has limited tax capacity and reinforced dependence on federal transfers.

Evidence Links
  • ³ U.S. GAO (1993) – Section 936 Tax Credit
  • ⁴ Abel & Deitz (2014) – Population & Economy
  • ⁵ BEA (2019) – Prototype Economic Statistics

Poverty, Inequality & Housing ⁶⁷⁸⁹

Poverty remains pervasive⁶, with child poverty above 50%. Median household income lags national levels⁷, while cost-burdened housing intensifies hardship⁸. One-third of residents live in areas highly susceptible to landslides⁹, underscoring a need for integrated social and environmental resilience.

Strategy Lens & Policy Outlook ¹⁰¹¹

Sustainable recovery depends on revitalizing locally anchored sectors, attracting talent, and strengthening human-capital pipelines. Evidence emphasizes that workforce reintegration, education modernization, and resilience investment must operate together to reverse demographic decline.
